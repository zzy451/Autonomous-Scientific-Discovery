## 4. Method and Workflow

### 4.1 Method motivation

The paper addresses a difficult nanophotonics search problem in which the design space is large and manual exploration is slow. The self-driving lab is meant to accelerate both performance optimization and scientific understanding of the emission-steering behavior.

### 4.2 Workflow sketch

1. Define a metasurface design space and an emission-steering target.
2. Use the active learning agent to choose the next experiment.
3. Build or configure the relevant structure and perform measurement.
4. Read out performance such as emission directivity.
5. Feed observations back into the learning loop.
6. Use equation / principle discovery to infer governing relationships.

### 4.3 System emphasis

The note should preserve both sides of the paper. On one side, it is a materials optimization platform.
